# Excel Content Strategy

Date: 2026-07-02

## Why This Cluster Matters

Excel and Google Sheets are natural entry points for real estate offices. Owners often start with a rent roll, rent payment tracker, expense sheet, owner report, or a combined operating file before they look for software.

This makes the Excel cluster valuable for three reasons:

- It matches real search intent: users search for templates before they search for software.
- It lets Qayd Al-Aqar meet the user where they are, without insulting the current workflow.
- It creates a practical bridge from spreadsheet records to an operating system: properties, leases, receivables, follow-ups, documents, and reports.

## Research Signals

Observed global content patterns:

- Rent roll templates are treated as a central real estate reporting artifact.
- Common spreadsheet use cases include rent roll, rent ledger, rent payment tracker, P&L, income/expense worksheet, maintenance log, and owner reporting.
- Spreadsheet limits repeat across sources: manual updates, version conflicts, missing reminders, weak audit trail, weak document context, and hard team collaboration.
- The strongest positioning is not "Excel is bad"; it is "Excel is a good start, but not enough for daily office operations."

## Future Expansion Rules

Add a new page only when it answers a distinct operational query:

- "ملف إكسل تحصيل الإيجارات" -> rent payment tracker guide/template.
- "شيت متابعة العقارات" -> property/unit inventory template.
- "تقرير مالك عقار Excel" -> owner report spreadsheet guide.
- "rent roll بالعربي" -> Arabic rent roll explainer and downloadable structure.
- "نقل بيانات العقارات من Excel" -> migration checklist and import preparation guide.

Avoid creating many thin template pages. Each page should explain:

1. What the spreadsheet does.
2. The minimum columns.
3. Common mistakes.
4. When the spreadsheet is enough.
5. When to move into Qayd Al-Aqar.
